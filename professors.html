Instructional and methodological strategies

Learning a foreign language can be very challenging. It not only demands the learning of new words and its meaning but also the rules by which those words can be combined to form meaninful phrases. To provide students with a meaningful learning experience, the present curriculum incorporates an interactive web interface, with the aim of effectively engage students in the learning of Spanish.

To reach this goal, the platform was conceived in accordance to the following instructional and methodological strategies:

  • It was developed using an intuitive design to avoid compromising user's cognitive resources and keep them focus on the task.
  • The intervention of the professor is minimum, which is determinant to stimulate students to gain independence and responsibility with their own learning process.
  • Each section is integrated by a lesson followed by interactive exercises that provide students with the opportunity to test their knowledge and get an instant feedback. This will help them monitor their learning and ultimately, to develop a metacognitive comprehension of their own learning process.
  • The exercises were designed as a ludic activity to boost students' motivation.
  • The platform was designed so professors could also keep track of the students' learning progress. This feature provides them with a tool for assessing and evaluating the students’ learning outcomes.
  • By switching between subjects, students can be in control of their own learning which is essential to enhance their motivation.
  • The first lessons use a semi-immersive approach to learning Spanish that will gradually transition to a fully immersive learning experience. In this way, we contribute to gradually build-up the student's self-confidence while maintaining their frustrations levels low.
  • The use of colors and images have the function of keeping the student's attention up and making learning a pleasant activity.
  • The exercises were designed to have users interacting with the content while learning. Indeed, kinesthetic activities have demonstrated to produce better learning outcomes since they provide students with a higher sensorial experience.

Goals

The class was designed to meet the following goals:

  1. Introduce students in the learning of Spanish as a foreign language.
  2. Enhance students' autonomy and independence in learning.
  3. Integrate new technologies in the school curriculum to boost student's media literacy and motivation for learning.

Learning Outcomes

After completed the lesson, the students must be able to:

  1. Idenify the characteristics of nouns, adjectives and articles
  2. Classify the noun according to class, gender and number
  3. Effectively conjugate nouns, adjectives and articles
  4. Elaborate simple phrases using nouns, articles and adjectives
